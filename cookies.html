1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

Similar technologies include:

  • Web beacons (also called pixel tags or clear GIFs): Small graphic images embedded in web pages or emails
  • Local storage: Data stored in your browser that persists even after you close the browser
  • Session storage: Data stored in your browser that is cleared when you close the browser tab
  • Device identifiers: Unique identifiers associated with your mobile device

This policy covers all these technologies, collectively referred to as "cookies."

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: To recognize you when you sign in and keep you logged in as you navigate our service
  • Security: To protect your account and detect suspicious activity
  • Preferences: To remember your settings and preferences (such as language and display preferences)
  • Progress tracking: To save your learning progress, streak data, and lesson completion status
  • Analytics: To understand how you use our service and identify areas for improvement
  • Performance: To monitor and improve the speed and performance of our service

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of our service. They enable core functionality such as security, authentication, and accessibility. You cannot opt out of these cookies as the service would not function without them.

Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your language preferences, display settings, and custom vocabulary lists. While not strictly necessary, disabling these may affect your experience.

Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our service by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve our service and identify technical issues.

Marketing Cookies

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant ads on other sites. We currently do not use marketing cookies, but if we do in the future, we will update this policy and obtain your consent where required.

We use services from the following third parties that may set cookies on your device:

We use Firebase for authentication, database, and analytics services. Firebase may set cookies to manage your session and collect analytics data. For more information, see Firebase Privacy Policy.

6. Cookies in Our Mobile App

Our mobile application uses similar technologies to cookies, including:

  • Local storage: To store your preferences and cached data for offline access
  • Device identifiers: For authentication and security purposes
  • Analytics SDKs: To collect anonymous usage data and improve the app

You can manage these through your device settings or within the app's settings menu.

7. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing cookies:

In-App Settings: You can manage analytics and non-essential cookies through the app's Settings > Privacy menu.

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete individual cookies or all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (note: this may prevent our service from working properly)
  • Set your browser to notify you when a cookie is being set

Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific third-party cookies using these links:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance Opt-out
  • Network Advertising Initiative Opt-out

8. Browser-Specific Instructions

Here's how to manage cookies in common browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage and delete cookies
  • iOS Safari: Settings > Safari > Clear History and Website Data
  • Android Chrome: Settings > Privacy > Clear browsing data

9. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want to be tracked. We currently respond to DNT signals by disabling non-essential analytics cookies when a DNT signal is detected.
